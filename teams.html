The National Basketball Association (NBA) consists of 30 teams, divided into two conferences: the Eastern Conference and the Western Conference. Each conference is further divided into three divisions, with five teams in each.

Eastern Conference

Atlantic Division:

Home to historic franchises like the Boston Celtics (17 championships) and the Philadelphia 76ers. Other teams: Brooklyn Nets, New York Knicks, and Toronto Raptors (2019 champions).

Central Division:

Features the Chicago Bulls, who dominated the '90s with Michael Jordan, and the Milwaukee Bucks (2021 champions). Other teams: Cleveland Cavaliers (2016 champions), Indiana Pacers, and Detroit Pistons.

Southeast Division:

Includes the Miami Heat (three-time champions), Atlanta Hawks, Washington Wizards, Charlotte Hornets, and Orlando Magic.

Western Conference

Northwest Division:

Led by the Denver Nuggets (2023 champions). Other teams: Minnesota Timberwolves, Portland Trail Blazers, Utah Jazz, and Oklahoma City Thunder.

Pacific Division:

Features the Los Angeles Lakers (17 titles) and the Golden State Warriors (4 championships from 2015 to 2022). Other teams: Phoenix Suns, LA Clippers, and Sacramento Kings.

Southwest Division:

Includes the legendary San Antonio Spurs (5 titles), Dallas Mavericks, Houston Rockets, Memphis Grizzlies, and New Orleans Pelicans.
